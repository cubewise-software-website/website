# Brand promise

Layer 1 — Brand. Loaded on every generation. Never changes mid-project.

---

## The one-sentence promise (internal)

> **Cubewise gives IBM Planning Analytics teams the professional software the platform was missing — built by people who actually use TM1, trusted by Finance and IT together.**

This is for the team. It doesn't appear anywhere on the site verbatim — it's the north star that every headline, hook and CTA is measured against.

---

## What the visitor should feel

**Finally, someone built this properly.**

Not *impressed* by features. Not *amazed* by technology. **Relieved.** The quiet recognition that someone who understands the problem has already solved it — and is not going to make a fuss about it.

| We aim for | Not |
|------------|-----|
| Relief | Excitement |
| Recognition ("they get it") | Novelty |
| Confidence | Aspiration |
| Trust | Hype |
| "This was made for me" | "This is impressive" |

## What Cubewise stands against

- **Marketing-speak that doesn't know TM1.** "Synergy," "empowerment," "transform your journey." The audience has heard it a thousand times.
- **Feature lists without benefit.** "Multi-dimensional writeback architecture" — nobody buys that. "Plan without fear" — yes.
- **Enterprise-software gatekeeping.** Contact forms before you can see a screenshot. Contract-only pricing. Demos you can't skip.
- **Pretending TM1 isn't 40 years old.** It is. That's the interesting part. The platform has survived four decades because it works. We make it *professional* — not trendy.
- **Treating Finance and IT as different species.** They share the same problem. Our software respects both.

---

## What Cubewise stands for

- **TM1 experts, not marketers.** Every line of copy should pass the "would a TM1 consultant nod at this?" test.
- **The DevOps way for IBM PA.** Modern software-engineering discipline, applied to a platform that never had it.
- **Finance-owned, IT-trusted.** Especially for Atmosphere and Slice — the Finance team is the user, but IT sleeps soundly.
- **Built together.** The platform is a system, not a catalogue. Arc is the hub; every other component extends or connects to it.
- **Show, don't gate.** Free downloads, real screenshots, actual testimonials with named customers and roles.
